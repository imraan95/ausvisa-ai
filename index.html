Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189)

What is the Skilled Independent Visa (189)?

The Subclass 189 is a permanent residence visa for skilled workers who are not sponsored by an employer, state/territory, or family member. It allows you to live and work anywhere in Australia.

What are the key requirements?

Key requirements include:

  • Age under 45
  • Competent English (IELTS 6 or equivalent)
  • Skilled occupation on the relevant skilled list
  • Positive skills assessment
  • Minimum 65 points score

Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190)

How does the 190 visa differ from 189?

The 190 visa requires state/territory nomination but provides additional points. You must live in the nominating state/territory for at least 2 years.

What are the state nomination criteria?

Each state/territory has its own criteria, which may include:

  • Specific occupations in demand
  • Higher English scores
  • Work experience requirements
  • Commitment to the state/territory

Employer Nomination Scheme (Subclass 186)

What are the streams available?

The 186 visa has three streams:

  • Direct Entry stream
  • Temporary Residence Transition stream
  • Labour Agreement stream
What are the employer requirements?

Employers must:

  • Be actively operating in Australia
  • Demonstrate genuine need for the position
  • Offer market salary rates
  • Meet training benchmarks

Temporary Skill Shortage (Subclass 482)

What is the visa duration?

The 482 visa duration varies:

  • Short-term stream: up to 2 years
  • Medium-term stream: up to 4 years
  • Labour Agreement stream: up to 4 years
What are the pathways to permanent residence?

482 visa holders may be eligible for permanent residence through:

  • Employer Nomination Scheme (186)
  • Skilled Independent Visa (189)
  • Regional pathways
